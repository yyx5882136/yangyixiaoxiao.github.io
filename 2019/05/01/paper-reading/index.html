Paper1: Variational Phase Imaging Using the Transport of Intensity Equation

2019/5/2

Abstract

  • 利用TIE相位恢复算法对透明目标成像
  • 做了仿真和真实数据实验

    Conclude

    TIE相位恢复的思路还是很重要的，通过建立差分方程，然后数值近似，利用焦平面和距离很近的非焦平面的强度来求解相位。需要思考和对比迭代相位恢复算法与TIE的优缺点。这篇文章的优化算法是我感兴趣的一类，利用ADMM求解，然后分别套用不同模型的已有的算法求解对应变量。实验部分，对于仿真数据很有借鉴意义，也是利用仿真模拟菲涅尔传播得到强度数据，这点可以被借鉴用来验证迭代型相位恢复算法，关于采用的仿真数据，提到了一个细胞图像数据库，或许可以提供我需要的先验。关于实验部分，可以看看Lei Tian大佬它们实验室的工作，已经开源代码和数据。

    Paper2: Reconstruction of Signals From Their Autocorrelation and Cross-Correlation Vectors, With Application to Phase Retrieval and Blind Channel Estimation

    2019/5/5

    Abstract

  • 相比于传统相位恢复利用信号的自相关来重构原信号，这篇文章考虑利用两个信号的自相关以及互相关四个观测值重构两个原信号，并且考虑模型在相位恢复和盲信道估计中的应用。
  • 优化求解算法还是信号提升的办法

    Conclude

    文章的附录部分是关于信号求解的保证证明，包括利用KKT条件求解等，值得参考和学习。

    Paper3: STFT Phase Retrieval: Uniqueness Guarantees and Recovery Algorithms

    2019/5/5

    Abstract

  • 我的摘要可以学习这么写：The problem of recovering a signal from its Fourier magnitude is of paramount importance in various fields of engineering and applied physics.Due to the absence of Fourier phase information, some form of additional information is required in order to be able to uniquely, efficiently, and robustly identify the underlying signal. Inspired by the application of fractional Fourier transform (FRFT) in optical imaging, we consider the problem of signal reconstruction from the FRFT magnitude. We first develop conditions under, which the FRFT magnitude is an almost surely unique signal representation. We then consider a semidefinite relaxation-based algorithm (FRliFT) and provide recovery guarantees. Numerical simulations complement our theoretical analysis and provide directions for future work.
  • 优化求解算法还是信号提升的办法

    Conclude

    如果想水文章的话，参照这篇文章的套路，拓展到分数傅里叶变换模型中去，便是一个可行的一篇水文。但我打算借鉴文章的写作套路以及目标，给出唯一性保证和提出重构算法，而算法本身想多一点创新性。但这篇文章最有价值的工作在于提供了唯一性保证的证明，这个是值得我去学习参考的内容。

    Paper4: Direct single-shot phase retrieval from the diffraction pattern of separated objects

    2019/5/5

    Abstract

  • 考虑多个目标的衍射模式，通过相位恢复算法，实现了single-shot重构信号，并在真实设备上做了实验验证
  • XFEL设备，需要单个衍射模式成像，多强度和TIE方法都不行。

    Conclude

    文章是相位恢复的一个重要应用，X射线成像，发到了nature communication上，它的参考文献大部分都是nature、science级别的，将来可以仔细看这些工作为相位恢复的应用提供线索。

    Paper5: Wide-field, high-resolution Fourier ptychographic microscopy

    2019/5/5

    Abstract

  • 这篇文章对我来说启发很大，可以说是验证了我另一个研究的可行性与前沿性。考虑的问题是Fourier ptychographic microscopy的高分辨率的问题，其中用到了相位恢复算法，采用的思路是倾斜成像平面获取更多冗余信息帮助恢复，在傅里叶域做的。这启发我们是否可以探究在不同成像平面，即菲涅尔域的冗余信息帮助相位恢复。
  • 这篇文章需要仔细研究，看看这个研究的细节，同时其参考文献以及后面引用这篇文献的工作都值得被顺藤摸瓜的看看，这是一个很insight的研究。

    Conclude

    文章的实验部分做的很夯实，有设备做实验很重要，需要考虑跟人合作来做这件事。

    Paper8: Undersampled Sparse Phase Retrieval via Majorization–Minimization

    2019/5/6

    Abstract

  • 同样是解决稀疏相位恢复问题，提出了两种算法，一种是利用Majorization-minimization算法重构稀疏信号，另一种是针对非稀疏信号，采用稀疏编码的思想，进行迭代重构

    Conclude

    这篇工作做的很充实，不仅回顾了之前所有稀疏相位恢复的工作，而且提出的算法也很有新颖性，对于第二种算法，其迭代优化的思路可能对我迭代换取凸性的想法有帮助，值得仔细推敲借鉴。其参考文献中涉及到了很多稀疏相位恢复的文章，是这条思路的一个脉络，同样的一些优化算法的文章比如ZQ Luo的工作，也值得学习参考。

    Paper9: On Two-Dimensional Hilbert Integral Equations, Generalized Minimum-Phase Signals, and Phase Retrieval

    2019/5/6

    Abstract

  • Present two-dimensional Hilbert Integral Equations, and apply them in phase retrieval. Simulation results demonstrating accurate reconstruction of 1-D and 2-D generalized minimum-phase signals from their magnitude spectra are presented to support the theoretical developments.

    Conclude

    I considered about using Hilbert transform into phase retrieval, but i found the difference between intensities and real part of signal, which makes it is impossible to retrieval accurately phase only given intensities. This paper demonstrates it is efficient for generalized minimum-phase signals, like exp(jwt). But i think i don’t understand this paper totally.

    Paper10: Gerchberg–Saxton algorithm applied in the fractional Fourier or the Fresnel domain

    2019/5/7

    Abstract

  • Apply Gerchberg-Saxton algorithm in the fractional Fourier or the Fresnel domain. Computer simulations illustrate its effiency.

    Conclude

    It is a natural idea that apply GS algorithm in fractional Fourier domain. But its simulations only in fresnel domain. In fact, fractional Fourier domain is related to fresnel domain. SO there is a question: why should we research the fractional Fourier phase retrieval instead of the fresnel phase retrieval? In another word, what is the difference between the fractional Fourier transform and fresnel integration and what is the advantage of frft applied in phase retrieval? I think frft’s convenient properties can answer these questions, like rotation additivity and fast algorithm.

    Paper12: Phase Retrieval via the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers

    Abstract

For the cost function obtained from them maximum likelihood criterion, we introduce auxiliary amplitude and phase variables to avoid the absolute value operator and decouple the determination of the auxiliary phase variables from that of the auxiliary amplitude variables.

    Conclude

    I had tried to apply this work in fractional Fourier phase retrieval, and the results of simulation was great. So there is a question: is there any disadvantage?

    Paper13: Signal Reconstruction From Two Close Fractional Fourier Power Spectra

    2019/5/7

    Abstract

  • Based on the definition of the instantaneous frequency (signal phase derivative) as a local moment of the wigner distribution, we derive the relationship between the instantaneous frequency and the derivative of the squared modulus of the fractional Fourier transform (fractional Fourier transform power spectrum) with respect to the angle parameter.

    Conclude

    The idea of paper is like TIE phase retrieval, but apply in the fractional Fourier domain. I think it is novel and creative. But I wonder what is it diadvantage or limits? How close between two fractional Fourier power spectra meet the applications? Copmpared with iterative phase retrieval algoritms, who is better on some perticular conditions?

    Paper16: prDeep: Robust Phase Retrieval with Flexible Deep Neural Networks

    2019/5/8

    Abstract

  • Phase retrieval (PR) algorithms have become an important component in many modern computational imaging systems. For instance, in the context of ptychography and speckle correlation imaging PR algorithms enable imaging past the diffraction limit and through scattering media, respectively. Unfortunately, traditional PR algorithms struggle in the presence of noise. Recently PR algorithms have been developed that use priors to make themselves more robust. However, these algorithms often require unrealistic (Gaussian
    or coded diffraction pattern) measurement models and offer slow computation times. These
    drawbacks have hindered widespread adoption.

    Conclude

    This paper may be a simply A+B work, but it opens a new world for me. I explore many work similar to my idea and I find this may be an interesting direction. I can combine the real application (like Fourier ptychography or biology imaging etc) and design an effient algorithm using deep learning and phase retrieval algorithms. What’s more, I can set a physical imaging model to generate database for my algorithm to learn.

    Paper20: Experimental robustness of Fourier Ptychography phase retrieval algorithms

    2019/5/9

    Abstract

  • Formulated the Fourier ptychographic phase retrieval problem using maximum likelihood optimization theory. Under this framework, they reviewed the existing FPM algorithms and classified them based on their cost function: amplitude-based algorithms and intensity-based algorithms. Because the noise and model mis-match error for brightfield and darkfield images depend on the measured intensity, the experimental results show amplitude-based cost function is better.

    Conclude

    The authors are big-fish in phase retrieval and optical imaging. Their work is worth to following and some code have been open source. I will have try them.

    Paper28: Deep learning approach to Fourier ptychographic microscopy

    2019/5/10

    Abstract

  • Fourier ptychographic microscopy: wide field-of-view and high resolution. For live cell imaging, need high speed to imaging. GAN

    Conclude

    This paper introduce some strategies for a particular challenge in applying DL to biomedical microscopy is the difficulty in gathering ground truth data needed for training the network, including synthetic data from simulations built with physical imaging models, semi-synthetic data that uses experimental data to guide simulations, experimental data captured with a different modality, and experimental data captured with the same modality. Here, they propose to use the traditional FPM reconstructed phase images as the ground truth for training.
